ESG Scoring Methodology

1960 stocks are rated for their wellness on each of the ESG pillars (Environmental, Social, and Governance) using 55 total different Bloomberg sourced fields of data. Those fields are aggregated and standardized according to maximum values to then rank the companies based on if the more or less is better for those particular values (more pollution emissions are worse, for example). All scores are then standardized from 0 to 100, where 100 signifies the best score. Below, the methods for calculating each one can be seen.

Environmental

Measures how well a company does in terms of environmental effects and consideration. The following is the makeup of this score:

  1. Pollution Emissions: 35%
  2. Policies and Initiatives: 15%
  3. Regulations Passed: 50%

Social

Measures how well a company does in terms of socially ethical topics such as bribery, health, safety, human rights, anti-slavery, etc. The following is the makeup of this score:

  1. Ethical Policies: 80%
  2. People Care/Protection: 20%

Governance

Measures how well a company does in terms of management in topics such as fairness, proper compensation, bias, diversity, etc. The following is the makeup of this score:

  1. Firm Commitment: 30%
  2. Diversity & Inclusion: 20%
  3. Fairness & Integrity: 50%

Total ESG

The overall/total ESG score considering all 3 pillars. It is just the average of the three pillar scores to get a sense of the overall ESG performance.

  1. Environmental: 1/3
  2. Social: 1/3
  3. Governance: 1/3
